Display Connectors and Cables

DVI interface -- digital visual interface
-single and dual link
	-single -- 3.7 Gbps (HDTV at 60 fps)
	-dual -- 7.4 Gbps (HDTV at 85 fps)
-DVI-A
	-analog signals
	-not generally used
-DVI-D
	-digital signals
-DVI-I
	-integrated -- digital and analog 
	-can be single or dual
-DVI is larger in connector type than HDMI or VGA

-Display Port
	-VESA standard (video electronics standards association)
		-royalty free standard -- no extra licensing cost
	-Data is sent in packetized form
		-like ethernet and PCI Express
	-compatible with HDMI and DVI
		-passive adapter
-RCA connector
	-designe by RCA in 1940's
	-phono connector
	-both video and audio (red, white (left audio), yellow (right audio) wires)
	-composite video
		-single RCA connector
		-one channel of analog video
		-yellow marking
		-often includes left (white) and right (red) audio connectors
	-component videos -- three RCA (YPbPr luminance and sync, blue, red)
	-orange -- digital audio
-VGA connector
	-video graphics array
	-DB-15 -- D-subminiature size B, 15 pin
		-actually size E
	-also called HD-15 or HD15
	-blue color - pc system design guide standard
	-only analog signal -- NO DIGITAL
-HDMI 
	-high definition multimedia interface
	-video and audio stream -- ALL DIGITAL, no analog
	-19 pin Type A connector (proprietary)
	-miniHDMI -- typc C connector designed for smaller devices
	-similar in size to display port but more symmetric
-BNC connectors
	-bayonet Neill-concelman
		-paul neill, carl concelman
		-bayonet is the connection at the end
	-used on higher-end video
		-maintain connectivity
	-component video
		-YPbPr
			-luminance and sync, blue, red
-MINI-DIN-4
	-S-Video (Separate Video)
	-2-channel analog signal
		-intensity and color
	-variants -- 7 and 9 -- change in number of pins
